Indego is the public bike sharing system that operates in Philadelphia. After several years of planning, Indego commenced operations with its first 60 stations and 600 bicycles in 2015. Currently, there are over 250 stations and more than 2,200 bicycles in operation. Over the past decade, more than 500,000 users adopted Indego as an easy, economical, and active way to travel in the city. Indego ridership has consistently risen annually since 2020, with total annual rides in 2023 surpassing one million for the first time in the program's history. This achievement was surpassed in 2024 with a record 1.3 million rides (Anon, 2025).     

The Philadelphia's bikeshare program claimed to be the first in the country to emphasise equity and specifically cater to disadvantaged people, including lower-income and minority individuals (Auchincloss et al., 2020).
