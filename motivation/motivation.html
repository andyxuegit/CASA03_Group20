Motivation

Most bike sharing programs indicate that the bikes are predominantly utilised by residents of elevated socioeconomic status (Auchincloss et al., 2020).

Previous studies indicate that bikesharing is predominantly utilised by white individuals whereas other demographics, including Black and Hispanic populations, tend to avoid the service (Biehl et al., 2018; Wang et al., 2016).

No literature studying the relationship between urban business networks and bike sharing networks.

To study the relationship between urban business networks and bike sharing networks

Whether the current Indego bike sharing networks and patterns have achieved this goal
