Conclusion and Suggestions

The results above show that the coverage of the Indego bikeshare system was limited to the business clusters surrounding the city centre, and the bikeshare network has not achieved its claim and ambition to specifically cater to disadvantaged people. 

It is suggested that the bikeshare system should be promoted for use in areas with a low proportion of white people, rather than merely being planned based on income levels (Caspi, 2023). 

Residents in low-income neighbourhoods may have insufficient awareness of the bikeshare system, partly because there is less coverage of stations in these areas. Therefore, increasing relevant publicity, education and community promotion activities is also a recommended strategy to enhance the usage rate (Caspi & Noland, 2019). 

Indego has implemented measures such as cash payment options and discounted pricing for food stamp holders, which should be continued. Also, more bikeshare stations could be built in north Philadelphia.
