Multidimensional Visualisation of Bikeshare Docking Station Service Buffer Zones

We create a service buffer zone with a radius of 400 meters for each docking station to analyse the relationship between annual trip numbers of each station in 2024 and business density, population density, median household income, and white population proportion in the service buffer zone.

The maximum distance that most users will walk to use the bikeshare is approximately one-quarter mile (~400 m) (LDA Consulting, 2015). Total annual trips of each station were obtained by adding outgoing trips and incoming trips according to the method in Caspi & Noland (2019). According to Caspi & Noland (2019) and Caspi (2023), Thiessen polygons were employed to allocate overlapping regions to the nearest station. The service area polygons show the spatial characteristics and help visualise the demographic and socioeconomic characteristics around each docking station.

Demographic and socioeconomic data on the block group level were modified through areal interpolation to determine the service area polygon values derived from the interpolation surface.
